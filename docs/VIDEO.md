# Circles — 3-minute demo video script

A shot-by-shot script for a ~3:00 recording. **VO** = what you say. **SCREEN** =
what you show/do. Record the demo on the live site (`vnmoorthy.github.io/circles`)
so it's obviously real. Aim to talk like a person, not a narrator.

**Total ≈ 3:00 · ~470 words of voiceover.**

---

### 0:00 – 0:18 · Hook  *(face cam or black slide, text: "the one you keep not having")*
**VO:** "There's a conversation you've been putting off. Coming out to a parent. Quitting a job you're loyal to. Telling someone you love that it's over. You rehearse it in the shower — and it falls apart the second they react in a way you didn't script."

### 0:18 – 0:32 · What it is  *(cut to Circles landing page)*
**VO:** "This is Circles. It's a self-directing agent loop that lets you *rehearse* the hard one. An agent plays the other person, watches where you break, and coaches you — looping until it decides you're ready."

### 0:32 – 1:05 · Set up a rehearsal  *(click "Coming out to a parent")*
**SCREEN:** Scenario opens; point at the right panel.
**VO:** "I'll pick coming out to a dad. On the right is the loop itself — plan, act, observe, self-correct — and a Readiness meter starting at zero. Watch it while I talk to him."
**SCREEN:** Type a *shaky, blaming* first line: *"You always make everything about you, so I don't even know why I'm telling you this."* Send.

### 1:05 – 1:35 · The observe + coach moment  *(persona replies defensively)*
**VO:** "See what happened — he got defensive, because I led with an attack. And the loop caught it: heat spiked, empathy dropped, it flagged the 'you always.' It's not scoring him — it's scoring *me*. And it tells me exactly how to fix it."
**SCREEN:** Point at the coaching card + the low readiness.

### 1:35 – 2:00 · Improve → readiness climbs  *(type a better line)*
**SCREEN:** Type: *"Dad, this is hard to say, and I love you. I'm gay — and I need you in my life."* Send.
**VO:** "Now I own it, I name the feeling, I make the ask. He softens — and the Readiness meter climbs. The loop is adapting his difficulty to how I'm doing."

### 2:00 – 2:22 · Throw a wrench  *(click a curveball, e.g. "They get angry")*
**VO:** "Here's my favorite part — I can throw a wrench. Let's make him suddenly angry, mid-conversation, and see if I can hold my line."
**SCREEN:** Send another line; persona reacts with the curveball; readiness wobbles then recovers.
**VO:** "That's the test — is your readiness real, or fragile?"

### 2:22 – 2:42 · Why it's a real loop  *(point at timeline + meter)*
**VO:** "This is a genuine loop, not a chatbot. Every round it re-scores me, adapts the difficulty, and updates readiness — and when it holds above eighty, it decides *on its own* that I'm ready. All of that logic is pure and unit-tested."

### 2:42 – 3:00 · Trust + close  *(cut to architecture slide, then live URL)*
**VO:** "The demo runs entirely in your browser — nothing you type leaves the device — with a privacy guard and Claude on AWS Bedrock behind it. It's live, it's open source, thirty-seven tests green. Everyone has a hard conversation coming. Circles is where you get it right — before it counts."
**SCREEN:** End on the URL: **vnmoorthy.github.io/circles**

---

## Recording tips
- Do the demo in one take on the live site; if the mock engine's reply isn't ideal, just re-send — it's deterministic per input.
- Keep the right-hand panel (Readiness + Loop timeline) visible the whole demo — that's the proof.
- The "throw a wrench" beat is the moment judges remember. Give it a breath.
- If you want a model-backed take, paste an Anthropic key in Settings first; otherwise the on-device engine is fine and safer for a recording.
